What has Singapore Polytechinic achieved?

What has it done in terms of environmental sustainability?

#1

Singapore prioritizes workforce upskilling for the green economy, aligning with sustainability goals. Workforce Singapore collaborates on a Career Conversion Programme (CCP) to nurture sustainability professionals, building a talent pipeline. A CCP for clean energy professionals, launched with Singapore Polytechnic, further supports this initiative.

Sustainability Objectives: Singapore's commitment to sustainability necessitates a skilled workforce to drive green initiatives, aligning with national environmental goals.

Economic Transition: As the global economy shifts towards sustainability, a skilled green workforce enhances Singapore's competitiveness in emerging industries..

Resource Efficiency: Equipping professionals with green skills optimizes resource use, reduces environmental impact, and supports a resilient and resource-efficient economy.

#2

Singapore Polytechnic (SP) and MolyWorks signed an MoU to advance metal additive manufacturing and recycling tech for the maritime sector, enhancing supply chain digitization and sustainability. The partnership entails promoting best practices, creating new alloys, and training industry professionals to implement the technologies. Witnessed by Senior Minister Chee Hong Tat, the agreement aims to reshape the maritime and offshore industry.

Maritime Industry Significance: Singapore's maritime economy necessitates metal tech focus, elevating competitiveness, innovation, and sustainability within this crucial industry..

Supply Chain Resilience: Advancing metal tech boosts maritime supply chain resilience by reducing imports, ensuring local component availability, and fostering self-sufficiency.

Environmental Commitment: Singapore's eco-friendly approach aligns with environmental responsibility, lowering carbon footprint, and driving greener maritime.

#3

Inchcape Singapore and Singapore Polytechnic (SP) collaborate through an MoU to educate and empower local talents in Electric Vehicles (EVs). The partnership aligns with SG Green Plan 2030, emphasizing EV readiness and sustainability. Co-designing advanced EV training and supplying resources, Inchcape Singapore and SP contribute to mobility's future.

Environmental Responsibility: In a compact urban setting, Singapore's sustainability is vital. Prioritizing EVs aligns with global climate efforts and enhances air quality.

Resource Constraints: Resource-scarce Singapore seeks energy independence by promoting EVs, reducing reliance on fossil fuels, and enhancing energy security through innovation.

Economic Competitiveness: Singapore's EV embrace fosters innovation, attracts clean energy investments, strengthens global competitiveness, and fuels long-term.

#4

Singapore's Samwoh collaborates with SP researchers and students to create durable road surfaces using plastic waste-infused asphalt. Initial testing reveals it can outlast regular asphalt by 20%, meeting chemical leaching guidelines. A pioneering effort, the project aims to contribute to sustainable construction practices and may soon be applied on roads.

Environmental Sustainability: Singapore's land scarcity and waste issues drive using plastic in roads for circular economy benefits and landfill reduction.

Resource Efficiency: Singapore's import reliance compels plastic waste recycling for roads, boosting resource efficiency and curbing environmental impact by conserving virgin materials.

Infrastructure ResilienceEnhancing road durability with plastic-infused asphalt boosts resilience, extending lifespan, reducing maintenance, and aiding cost-effective.
